# Security Policy: Responsible Disclosure

## 1. Overview

Security is a core design objective of the **TRV™ Cryptographic Engine (TRVEngine™)**.

TRV™ Labs welcomes independent cryptanalysis, technical evaluation, and responsible security research related to:
- the **Boolean Transformation Gate System (BTGS)**,
- TRV™ functional configurations,
- implementation behavior,
- and operational security characteristics.

We are committed to coordinated disclosure practices that support the continued resilience and integrity of the TRV™ ecosystem.

---

## 2. Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a potential vulnerability, cryptographic weakness, implementation flaw, side-channel issue, or unexpected security behavior, please report it privately before any public disclosure.

### Security Contact

**TRV™ Labs**  
security@trvengine.com

Please include:
- A detailed description of the issue
- The affected component or operational mode
- Reproduction steps or proof-of-concept
- Technical impact assessment, if available
- Relevant logs, traces, or test vectors where applicable

---

## 3. Coordinated Disclosure Process

TRV™ Labs follows a Coordinated Responsible Disclosure model.

### 1. Acknowledgment
Reports will generally be acknowledged within **48–72 hours**.

### 2. Investigation
A technical investigation and validation process will be performed to evaluate the findings.

### 3. Resolution
If confirmed, appropriate mitigations, patches, or corrective actions will be developed and tested.

### 4. Coordinated Public Disclosure
Public disclosure may be coordinated with the reporting researcher after remediation or mitigation has been completed and verified.

---

## 4. Safe Harbor

Researchers acting in good faith under this policy are considered authorized for the limited purpose of vulnerability discovery and responsible disclosure.

TRV™ Labs will not pursue legal action against researchers who:
- comply with this Security Policy,
- conduct analysis for non-commercial informational purposes,
- avoid privacy violations, service disruption, or data destruction,
- do not exploit vulnerabilities beyond verification purposes,
- and do not publicly disclose unresolved vulnerabilities prior to coordination.

---

## 5. Coordinated Disclosure Requirement

Public disclosure of unresolved vulnerabilities, exploit tooling, or active attack methodologies prior to coordination with TRV™ Labs may be considered outside the scope of this policy.

---

## 6. Out-of-Scope Issues

This policy does not generally cover:
- brute-force attacks against weak or intentionally insecure keys,
- vulnerabilities requiring physical compromise of host systems,
- operating system or third-party environment vulnerabilities,
- denial-of-service scenarios caused by unrealistic hardware constraints,
- social engineering attacks unrelated to TRV™ itself.

---

## 7. Security Disclaimer

TRV™ is an independently developed cryptographic system undergoing ongoing public analysis and adversarial evaluation.

No guarantee is provided regarding:
- resistance to cryptanalysis,
- suitability for production deployment,
- compliance with external standards,
- or immunity from implementation vulnerabilities.
